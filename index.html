Dona's Story

In February 2025, Dona was diagnosed with Stage 3 Ovarian Cancer. Thanks to good medical insurance, she began chemotherapy right away, followed by surgery that July. But the results weren't what she and her doctors had hoped for — her oncologist estimated that further traditional chemotherapy would add only six months to a year to her life.

Dona went searching for another way forward. She found a treatment being used successfully on patients with similar diagnoses — some at Stage 4 — who are still doing well four years later. To her, the choice was clear. The obstacle is that her insurance will not cover this treatment, even though some of the medications involved are FDA approved.

"I am a young at heart 65 year‑old woman with a lot more life to live, given the opportunity."
— Dona Carbone

Dona isn't asking for herself alone. For years she has given her time to others — travelling from Long Island into Manhattan multiple times a week to bring food and support to people who are homeless or in need. Now her church family and friends are asking you to help give back to her.

A Lifetime of Serving Others

Ordained in 1993, Dona had already been volunteering as a worship leader at Love and Mercy Fellowship since 1987. In the decades since, her service has touched nearly every part of church and community life.

Board of Deacons
